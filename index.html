OUR SERVICES

Cosmetic Dentistry

Teeth Whitening: Removing stains and discoloration to brighten teeth.
Veneers: Thin shells of porcelain or composite resin placed over the front surface of teeth to improve appearance.
Bonding: Applying a tooth-colored resin to repair chips, cracks, or gaps in teeth.
Orthodontics: Straightening teeth using braces, clear aligners, or other devices.

Oral Surgery

Tooth Extractions: Removing damaged, decayed, or impacted teeth.
Implant Placement: Placing dental implants in the jawbone as a foundation for replacement teeth.
Biopsies: Removing a small section of tissue for testing if oral cancer or other conditions are suspected.

Pediatric Dentistry

Child Dental Care: Specialized care for children's teeth, including preventive treatments like sealants and fluoride applications.
Space Maintainers: Devices used to maintain space for permanent teeth after premature loss of baby teeth.

Emergency Dental Care

Pain Relief: Treating severe toothaches or dental abscesses.
Broken Teeth: Repairing or replacing chipped, cracked, or knocked-out teeth.
Dental Trauma Care: Managing injuries to the mouth and teeth resulting from accidents.

Periodontal (Gum) Care

Scaling and Root Planing: Deep cleaning procedures to treat gum disease by removing plaque and tartar from below the gumline.
Gum Surgery: Surgical procedures to treat advanced gum disease or reshape the gums.

Specialized Services

Oral Cancer Screening: Checking for signs of oral cancer during routine exams.
TMJ/TMD Treatment: Addressing issues related to the temporomandibular joint (jaw joint) and associated muscles.
Sleep Apnea Treatment: Providing dental appliances to help manage obstructive sleep apnea.

WORK PROCESS

Preventive and Diagnostic Care

Preventive Services: Focused on maintaining oral health and preventing dental issues. This includes regular check-ups, teeth cleaning, fluoride treatments, and patient education on oral hygiene.

Diagnostic Services: Involves identifying dental problems through examinations, X-rays, and other diagnostic tools. This step helps in early detection of issues like cavities, gum disease, and oral cancer.

Treatment and Restorative Care

Restorative Services: Address existing dental problems. This includes procedures like fillings, crowns, root canals, and extractions to repair damaged teeth and restore oral health.

Cosmetic Treatments: Enhancements for aesthetic purposes, such as teeth whitening, veneers, and orthodontics, which improve the appearance of teeth and smile.

Post-Treatment and Follow-Up Care

Post-Treatment Care: Involves providing patients with instructions on how to care for their teeth after procedures, along with managing pain and ensuring proper healing.

Follow-Up and Maintenance: Regular follow-up appointments and ongoing monitoring to ensure the success of treatments and to maintain long-term oral health. This may include scheduling routine check-ups and additional care as needed.
